Monday Chronology - India Only

Think of Monday as a checklist day. The bot does not wake up and buy stocks. It first prepares research, asks you to approve a short watchlist, waits for live price confirmation, then proposes one trade. A real order happens only after human unlock.

Bucket 1: Safety

Before looking for trades, the bot checks whether trading is even allowed.

  • No kill switch file.
  • Monday is not an NSE holiday.
  • India VIX is below 20.
  • Daily loss has not hit Rs 300.

Bucket 2: Stock Filtering

The bot starts with Nifty 50 only, then narrows to stocks with a fresh reason to move.

  • Fresh news or sector catalyst.
  • Clean momentum or pullback setup.
  • Good enough volume to avoid sleepy names.
  • Only 3 to 5 candidates are sent to you.

Bucket 3: Human Control

You decide what can be traded that day. No approval means no trade.

  • You approve RELIANCE, HDFCBANK, etc.
  • Rejected names are blocked.
  • Approved names still need live entry gates later.

Read the rulebook

Load India strategy, trade log, research log, approved watchlist, weekly review, proposals, and NSE holidays. The active India capital is Rs 20,000, so daily loss cap is Rs 300 and kill switch is around Rs 3,000 drawdown from peak.

08:45

Call the VIX wrapper. If VIX is 20 or higher, the day becomes watch-only. If the kill switch exists, all new entries are blocked.

08:50
Build candidate list

Scan Nifty 50 names for fresh catalysts. Example: RELIANCE has strong energy news, HDFCBANK has banking momentum, SUNPHARMA has pharma rotation.

09:00
Ask you for approval

The bot sends 3 to 5 candidates. If you reply Y1 Y3, only those names become tradeable. If you reply NONE or do not reply, the rest of the day has no entries.

09:20
Look for live confirmation

Now the bot watches only approved stocks. It checks early price action, volume, breakout or pullback quality, VIX, open position count, cost cap, and daily P&L.

09:25+
Propose, then wait

If one setup is strongest, the bot proposes quantity, entry, stop, T1, and T2. It still cannot place the order until you unlock that exact trade.

After Entry: Continue vs Exit

Trade Continues

RELIANCE is bought at Rs 1,800 with stop at Rs 1,715. Price moves to Rs 1,870. This is profitable, but below T1. The bot keeps the position open and watches it during pulse checks.

  • If profit reaches about +0.8R, stop can move up to breakeven.
  • The stop can only move up, never down.
  • No exit happens just because the trade is mildly green.

Partial Win

Price reaches T1 at Rs 1,927.50. The bot closes 50% and moves the stop to entry. Now the worst case on the remaining piece is roughly flat.

  • T1 means bank some profit.
  • Remaining quantity is protected.
  • If price later reaches T2, the rest is trailed.

Trade Exits

If price falls to Rs 1,715, the stop exits the trade. If a thesis-break appears, the bot flags watch-only and asks for human action. If it is 15:15 IST, MIS must be squared off.

  • Stop hit: exit.
  • 15:15 IST: exit all MIS.
  • Kill switch: flatten everything and stop trading.

Feedback Loop: How The Bot Learns Safely

The bot does not secretly change rules after a loss or win. It collects evidence, writes notes, scores itself, and proposes improvements. You decide whether any rule actually changes.
Exit
Write a post-mortem

After every closed trade, the bot records what happened: entry quality, management quality, research quality, discipline, and whether the result was skill or luck.

Example
A losing RELIANCE trade

Suppose RELIANCE had a catalyst, but volume faded after entry and it closed near the stop. The post-mortem may say: research quality 4/5, entry quality 2/5, discipline 5/5, adjustment candidate: require stronger first-5-minute volume before entry.

Daily
Score the day

The daily summary computes rolling 5-trade and 20-trade metrics: win rate, average R, profit factor, discipline, and whether gates were respected.

Weekly
Turn patterns into proposals

If several trades show the same weakness, the bot writes a proposal in STRATEGY-PROPOSALS.md. Example: Add rule that long entries need first-5-minute volume above 1.5x average.

Human
You approve or reject

If you accept, you commit the change into TRADING-STRATEGY.md. If you reject, the bot cannot keep nagging immediately because cooldown and rejection-suppression rules apply.

Feedback Example 1

Five trades entered after open gaps all faded. The bot proposes: wait for a pullback or opening-range hold instead of buying the first spike.

Feedback Example 2

Three winning trades reached +0.8R then reversed. The bot may propose tightening the stop earlier, but only if enough trade evidence exists.

Feedback Example 3

If discipline is perfect but results are weak, the issue may be the setup filter. If discipline is poor, the issue is rule-following, not the strategy.
